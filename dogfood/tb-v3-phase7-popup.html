TB v3 Phase 7 v2 — 3D Popup

Founder smoke test · 8 steps · run against http://localhost:3131 with the dev server running

Step 1 of 8

Empty-canvas 3D popup opens

Open the Network Builder (TB v3). Leave the canvas completely empty — no devices, no cables. Click the 3D pill in the modebar.

Expected: A modal overlay appears centred on screen. It has a header showing "3D view of topology", a counts line reading "0 devices · 0 cables", and an X close button. The viewport area shows an empty-state message (e.g. "Add devices to the canvas to see them in 3D."). The rest of the canvas is dimmed behind a blurred backdrop.
Step 2 of 8

Devices render as extruded 3D cards

Close the popup (X button or Esc). Drop 3 devices onto the canvas — e.g. a router, a switch, and a workstation. Re-open the 3D popup.

Expected: Counts update to "3 devices · 0 cables". Each device appears as a bronze extruded card in the 3D viewport — a top face showing the device icon and label, four side rims, and a bottom face. Cards sit on a floor with a radial vignette. The viewport has perspective depth (not flat).
Step 3 of 8

Drag rotates the scene

With the 3D popup open and devices visible, click and drag inside the viewport — left/right and up/down.

Expected: The scene rotates in 3D around the Y axis (horizontal drag) and X axis (vertical drag). Rotation is smooth, proportional to drag distance, and stays within the vertical clamp (15° – 75° pitch). The floor and device cards rotate together.
Step 4 of 8

Momentum decays after drag release

Drag quickly across the viewport and release the mouse button while still moving.

Expected: The scene continues to rotate briefly after release, then decelerates and stops (Emil-tuned 0.92/0.94 decay). It does not snap to a stop immediately on mouseup, and it does not spin indefinitely. Decay happens within roughly 300–500 ms.
Step 5 of 8

Scroll wheel zooms in and out

With the 3D popup open, scroll the mouse wheel up and down inside the viewport.

Expected: Scrolling up zooms in (scene grows), scrolling down zooms out (scene shrinks). Zoom stays within sensible bounds — it cannot zoom in so far that all geometry disappears, nor zoom out so far that nothing is visible. Page does not scroll while the wheel is over the viewport.
Step 6 of 8

Double-click resets camera

Rotate the scene and zoom in or out so it is clearly off-default. Then double-click anywhere inside the viewport.

Expected: The scene animates back to the default camera position (rotX ≈ 30°, rotY ≈ −20°, zoom ≈ 1.0) over roughly 320–400 ms. The tween is smooth — not a snap. After the tween completes, dragging works normally from the reset position.
Step 7 of 8

Live sync — canvas edit reflects in open popup

Keep the 3D popup open. In a separate browser tab (or by temporarily dismissing via Esc and immediately reopening), add a cable between two devices on the canvas, then re-open the popup without closing it first. Alternatively: open the popup, then in the browser console run window._certanvilFeatures['topology-builder-v3']._renderCanvas() after mutating state to add a device programmatically.

Expected: The device count and 3D scene update to reflect the new topology state. A device added while the popup is open appears in the 3D viewport without requiring a close-and-reopen. Counts header updates accordingly.
Step 8 of 8

Esc closes cleanly + reduced-motion fallback

With the popup open, press Escape. Then enable System Preferences → Accessibility → Reduce Motion (or use DevTools → Rendering → Emulate prefers-reduced-motion: reduce) and re-open the popup.

Expected (Esc): The popup closes immediately. Focus returns to the canvas or the 3D modebar pill. The modal DOM node is removed. The 3D mode pill is no longer in its active/pressed state.

Expected (reduced-motion): The popup opens instantly — no fade-in or scale animation. Drag still rotates the scene (motion driven by input, not CSS animation), but momentum decay is disabled (scene stops immediately on mouseup).
