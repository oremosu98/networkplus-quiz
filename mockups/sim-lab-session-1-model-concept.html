One sim and done feels thin. How should rounds work?

Today a sim is a single scenario, then back to Home. Its siblings run multiples: Reword Gauntlet is five rungs, Why-Not scores four reasons. The real decision is how a multi-round session splits across Free and Pro.

A Free short session, Pro goes long and unlimited Recommended

Free

Pick 3 or 5 rounds, one session a day. The session is your free sim.

Pro

Unlimited sessions, exam-length runs up to 10, weak-spot tracking across rounds.

Why this one: it mirrors the free Reword Gauntlet (five rungs, one run a day), so all three drills feel like the same product. Free gets richer; Pro earns its price on length plus tracking, not on locking you out.

B Keep one free scenario, multi-round is the Pro hook

Free

One single scenario a day. Exactly today's behaviour.

Pro

Multi-round Exam-mode sessions plus the analytics.

Why this one: the original Plan 1 / Plan 2 split, the most conservative on AI cost. The downside is the one you just felt: Free stays one-and-done.

C A fixed 3-round session for everyone

Free

A set three-round session, one a day. No length picker.

Pro

Unlimited sessions plus the analytics.

Why this one: simplest to build and explain. But it drops the part you asked for, choosing how many rounds.

Note on the daily cap: with A or C, "1 free sim a day" becomes "1 free session a day", so a session of N rounds still counts as the single daily free. That keeps the Pro upsell intact and the paywall copy honest.
