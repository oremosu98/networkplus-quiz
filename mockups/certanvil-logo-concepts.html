🔨 CertAnvil — Logo & Wordmark Concepts

Six visual directions for the CertAnvil brand mark + wordmark. Each concept includes a hero presentation (dark + light theme), an in-context sidebar preview matching the live app, and engineering specs (SVG paths, colors, fonts) ready to drop into Figma or directly into the codebase. Click any concept's icon to inspect the SVG. Recommendation flagged at the end.
Brand brief — what we're solving for Parent-product brand for an AI cert-prep platform. Currently houses Network+ + Security+; will scale to AZ-900, CCNA, AWS, Azure. Audience is IT cert candidates (mostly individuals, some small training shops). Founder story: tool was built and used to pass Network+ N10-009 with a 767/900 in 8 weeks. Brand DNA: forge / craft / hammer-and-anvil — you forge yourself into a passer using the tool.

My Recommendation

★Concept 03 — CA Monogram

Recommended
Strongest "modern SaaS" identity. Scales perfectly from 16px favicon to 56px feature. Pairs cleanly with the existing app's --accent: #7c6ff7 palette so no other CSS needs to change. The narrative loss vs. literal anvil concepts is real, but reclaimed via the wordmark ("CertAnvil") — the meaning lives in the name, the mark just badges it.

Why I'd pick this:
  • Perfect favicon legibility at 16×16 (literal anvils get muddy)
  • Matches Vercel / Linear / Anthropic / Stripe app-icon discipline
  • Already coordinates with your existing accent palette — drop-in compatible
  • Easiest to refine in Figma — pure geometry, no detailed illustration
  • Works in 2-color (purple + amber spark) AND single-color contexts
If you want narrative weight Concept 01 The Anvil is the strong runner-up. The literal anvil is more memorable in marketing contexts (social shares, demo videos). Trade-off: harder favicon. You can use both — Concept 03 for the app/favicon, Concept 01 for marketing surfaces. Many tech brands run dual-identity systems (Vercel uses different marks in different contexts).

Figma Migration · Engineering Handoff
How to use these concepts in Figma Right-click any logo above → "Inspect Element" → copy the <svg> markup → paste into Figma (it accepts inline SVG via paste). All paths, fills, gradients translate directly. Resize, recolor, rearrange there.
How to use these concepts in the app codebase The SVG markup above can drop directly into renderAppSidebar() in app.js — just replace the <div class="sb-brand-mark">CA</div> with the SVG of your chosen concept. Sidebar mark is currently 38×38 px; the concepts are designed for that size. Once you pick a direction we'll refactor to render the chosen concept's SVG at boot.
Favicon generation Once you pick a concept, generate favicon.ico, apple-touch-icon.png (180×180), and icon-192.png / icon-512.png (PWA manifest). realfavicongenerator.net takes one PNG and produces the full set. Concept 03 is the best fit for this — the rounded square scales cleanly to all required sizes.
Design rationale summary Six concepts span the design space from literal-craft (Concept 01 Anvil) through active-builder (Concept 02 Hammer Strike) to modern-SaaS (Concept 03 Monogram) to technical-engineering (Concept 04 Blueprint) to typography-only (Concept 05 Wordmark) to institutional-heritage (Concept 06 Seal). My recommendation is Concept 03 because: (a) you're building a SaaS not a guild, (b) the favicon/app-icon story matters more than the marketing-asset story for a solo founder, (c) the wordmark "CertAnvil" carries enough narrative weight that the icon doesn't need to also tell the forge story.

All concepts are vector SVG, scalable, and ready to refine in Figma or drop directly into the codebase. Pick one — or pick two and run a hybrid identity system. No bad answers.
