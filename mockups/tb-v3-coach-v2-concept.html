When you added R-Home Routers move packets between subnets. R-Home is now sitting on both your LAN and the ISP link. That's the gateway role.
Q
Why does the SOHO router need to face the ISP, not the other way around?
Coach

On your canvas, R-Home has two sides. The LAN side faces PC-1 and Phone-1. The WAN side faces the ISP. Direction matters because NAT runs on the WAN interface, not the LAN.

When PC-1 reaches the internet, R-Home rewrites its private source address to the public IP the ISP gave you. Replies come back to that public IP and R-Home translates them back to PC-1. If you wired it the other way, replies would land on the LAN side with no NAT table entry, so nothing gets back to the host.

For the exam: NAT direction sits on the WAN, the public IP lives there, and your default gateway points at the same interface. Those three are tested together.

Why it works
01

Forged-bronze accent, nothing else.

Zero purple. Zero violet wash. Every accent surface reads from the same --accent token the home masthead and analytics page use. Borders are hairline color-mix tints, not rgba shadows. Tokens come straight from home-masthead-v3-concept.html.

02

Editorial type voice, no badge chip.

The "CA / Coach / 1 today" chip and the bot-style avatar are gone. The header now reads like a magazine section: a Fraunces title, an accent eyebrow with a rule. The session counter is a small monospace line in the footer.

03

Three real states, not one.

Free Build shows you what the panel looks like when there's nothing to say, when it's thinking, and when it has an answer. The empty state explains what to do next. The loading state is a skeleton, not a spinning gear. The answered state cites the devices on your canvas by hostname.

04

Hints feel like a clipboard, not a chat.

PBQ hints are a numbered list. Used hints fill the pip with the accent. The AI escape pip is dashed until it activates, then solid — a small visible signal that you've crossed a boundary. No chat bubbles, no avatars, no "thinking…" copy floating in space.

05

Touch targets and focus rings that exist.

Every interactive control has a 44×44px minimum hit area and a visible 2px accent ring on :focus-visible. The input is a real <label for="">. Hover transforms are gated behind @media (hover: hover) so touch devices don't fire false hovers. prefers-reduced-motion keeps opacity transitions and drops everything else.

06

The AI is told to act like a person.

Plain text only. No markdown headers. Cite a device on the student's canvas by hostname every time. No "Great question!", no "Let me help with that". The sample answer above names R-Home, PC-1, and Phone-1 directly because the canvas state was in the prompt and the system prompt forced the tutor to reach for it.
