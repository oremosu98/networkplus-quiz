What is the core flaw in this architecture?

Correct. Everything rides on one control. Once the perimeter is bypassed, by phishing or an insider, nothing else slows the attacker before the data.

Look past the firewall. There are no endpoint, data, or identity controls, so a single bypass reaches the crown jewels unopposed.

Build the inner layers, and classify one control.

Right. EDR and hardening give each host its own line of defense.

Correct answer: EDR + host hardening. Each endpoint should defend itself, not rely on the edge.

Right. Encryption protects the records if they are reached; DLP catches them leaving.

Correct answer: encryption at rest + DLP. Protect the data itself, not just the path to it.

Right. MFA and least privilege shrink what a stolen credential can do.

Correct answer: MFA + least privilege. The shared admin account is a single key to everything.

Right. MFA is enforced by technology (technical) and stops misuse before it happens (preventive).

Correct answer: technical, preventive. It is enforced by a system and acts before an event, not after.
