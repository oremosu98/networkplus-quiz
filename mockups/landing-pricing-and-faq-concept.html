Two ships from the cert-mode quick-wins menu. Pricing page is a standalone /pricing route with the 3-tier comparison, pass-guarantee recap, and a small "Pricing-related FAQ" subset. FAQ section is inline on the main landing page (between Why CertAnvil and Footer) using native <details> accordion. Pricing structure confirmed from product-vision docs: USD primary, Free $0 forever (50 Q/day), Pro $14.99/mo or $89/yr (save 50%), Team Coming Soon. Annual prominent. Compare-style answer in the FAQ stays category-level ("static prep tools / question banks") — no individual competitors named.

Architectural decisions (handshake)

1. /pricing is a separate route, not a section on landing. Topbar already has a "Pricing" link routing to #; we fill it. Separate route lets future referral links / Reddit posts / anywhere external point at "the pricing page" cleanly. Self-contained landing/pricing.html file with shared topbar/footer markup duplicated (no SSI in static-only Vercel; pure duplication is the pragmatic choice for two pages).

2. FAQ section is INLINE on landing only — not a separate /faq route. Visitors who reach FAQ are still in the landing-funnel; making them click through a route would lose them. Native <details>/<summary> for the accordion (zero JS, accessible by default, browser handles open/close).

3. Pricing page also gets a small FAQ subset — 4 of the most pricing-relevant questions (cancel anytime, refunds, BYOK status, pass guarantee mechanics). Avoids forcing pricing-curious visitors back to the landing for the answers.

4. Pro tier visually elevated: thicker accent border + "MOST POPULAR" pill + slight Y-translate. Industry standard. Hierarchy = Free / Pro / Team — not equal weight.

5. Annual prominent on Pro card: $89/yr displayed at the top with "Save 50%" green pill; monthly price ($14.99) shown as secondary "or $14.99/mo". This drives annual sign-ups (better cash flow, lower churn).

6. Pass-guarantee recap on pricing page: small green-shielded section after the tier grid. Reuses v2 fraud-conscious copy verbatim — no duplicated commitment, just a recap with a "Read more →" link to the landing-page section. Critical because visitors landing on /pricing directly (e.g. from a referral link) might not have seen the landing pass-guarantee section yet.

7. Topbar nav update: add "Pricing" between "Certs" and "Sign in" / theme toggle. Currently no Pricing link exists in production. The link is the visible nav-pivot — without it visitors don't know there's a pricing page.

8. FAQ comparison stays category-level, not vendor-named (revised): instead of singling out Dion Training, the comparison frames CertAnvil vs the broader category of "static prep tools / question banks". Reasons: (a) avoids PR/legal friction with named competitors, (b) doesn't look petty or aggressive, (c) covers ALL static-bank competitors (Dion, CertMaster, Mike Myers, Kaplan, Boson, ExamCompass) in one comparison, (d) doesn't go stale if any single competitor pivots their model. The table still has 5 differentiating rows; just the column header changes from "Dion Training" → "Static prep tools".

Can I cancel anytime? ▼
Yes — Pro is month-to-month or annual, cancel from your account dashboard. Annual cancellations stop the renewal but keep your Pro access through the period you've already paid for.
Do I need my own AI/API key? ▼
No. The Pro subscription covers the AI cost on our side — your monthly fee includes Claude API tokens. Free tier likewise. Zero "bring your own key" complexity.
What's covered by the pass guarantee? ▼
Pro users who use the platform actively for 30+ days, complete a mock exam, and don't pass on their first attempt at the real CompTIA exam. Submit your Pearson VUE booking + score report; we extend your Pro access until you pass. Manual review.
Is there a refund policy? ▼
14-day money-back if Pro isn't right for you. After that, the pass guarantee is the active "we'll back you up" mechanism. We'd rather over-deliver on a few cases than under-deliver on the brand promise.

Can I really study for free? ▼

What's a Performance-Based Question (PBQ)? ▼
CompTIA's hands-on exam format — drag/drop, command-line simulation, network topology configuration. Way more weight on the real exam than MCQs (worth ~40% of your score). Most prep tools skip them; CertAnvil includes a CLI sim, topology builder, and ACL builder right in Pro.
How does the pass guarantee work? ▼
Pro users who put in the work — 30+ days active platform use, completed mock exam, maintained streak — and don't pass on first attempt get their Pro access extended free of charge until they do. Submit your Pearson VUE booking confirmation + score report; manual case review within 7 days.
Do I need my own AI / API key? ▼
No. Pro subscription includes the AI cost. Free tier likewise. We pay Claude API; you pay one subscription. Zero "bring your own key" complexity.
What if my exam isn't listed yet? ▼
Click "Notify me" on any "Coming soon" cert tile and we'll email you the moment it goes live. Current pipeline: Network+ (live), Security+ (Q3 2026), Azure AZ-900, Cisco CCNA, AWS SAA, Azure AZ-104.
Can I cancel anytime? ▼
Yes — Pro is month-to-month or annual, cancel from your account dashboard. Annual cancellations stop the renewal but keep your Pro access through the period you've already paid for.
How do you avoid recycled textbook content? ▼
Every question is AI-generated fresh, then run through a 7-layer validation pipeline (prompt self-check, exemplar bank reference, AI second-pass on Sonnet, ground-truth tables, programmatic validators, regression audit, Sonnet escalation). The bank reference is 320+ hand-curated original exemplars per cert — zero ingestion of paid textbook publishers (Dion, CertMaster, Mike Myers, Kaplan). Legal-clean, blueprint-anchored.
