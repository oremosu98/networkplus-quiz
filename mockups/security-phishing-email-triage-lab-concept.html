🧬
Anatomy of a phish — universal flags
1. Sender mismatch Display-name says one thing, email/number/sender-ID says another. "Microsoft" <noreply@suspicious.tk>
2. Urgency cue "ACT NOW", "24 hours", "final notice." Designed to short-circuit verification.
3. Isolation / secrecy "Don't tell anyone", "between us", "I'm in a meeting." Stops you asking the real person.
4. Lookalike URL / domain Typosquat (microsoft.com vs mlcrosoft.com), recent registration, IP-instead-of-domain.
5. Generic greeting "Dear Customer", "Hi User", or template-style first-name only. Templates beget mass-send.
6. Suspicious attachment / payload .zip (often password-protected to bypass scanners), .iso, .exe, macros enabled.
7. Pre-empts verification "I can't be reached by phone", "don't reply to this address", "use the form below." Closes alternative channels.
8. Money or credentials ask Wire transfer, gift card, password reset, MFA prompt. The point of the entire attack.
📧
Email phish — vector-unique flags
  • Reply-To ≠ From — replies are routed to attacker's inbox
  • Display-name spoof — anyone can set "From" display name to "your CEO"
  • Hover URL ≠ visible URL — link text says one thing, href says another
  • Header anomalies — SPF/DKIM/DMARC failures (advanced; usually behind-the-scenes)
  • BEC → wire fraud is the #1 financial-loss phish category
💬
Smishing — vector-unique flags
  • Custom sender ID for unsolicited fraud-alerts (legit banks use your registered shortcode)
  • Shortened URLs (bit.ly, tinyurl) hide the real destination — never tap blind
  • Reply-keyword fishing ("YES/NO") — reply confirms your number is live + reachable
  • Callback number not on physical card — verify against the back of your debit card, not the SMS
  • Tighter time pressure than email (30 min instead of 24 hr)
📞
Vishing — vector-unique flags
  • Caller-ID spoofing is trivial — assume any incoming number can be faked
  • Recorded message + "press 1" — robocall pattern
  • Tax/IRS/Medicare impersonations are seasonal favourites
  • "Microsoft / Apple support" — neither company calls you about your account
  • Verify via known number from the back of your card or the official website — never the number in the voicemail
📱
Quishing — vector-unique flags
  • Physical sticker over original — common at parking meters, restaurants, vending
  • Decode URL before tapping — most camera apps preview the URL on hover
  • Recent domain registration — public WHOIS check; phish domains often <30 days old
  • HTTPS ≠ trustworthy — Let's Encrypt issues for free; padlock means "encrypted in transit", not "legit"
  • Use official app instead — when in doubt, install the org's app from the App Store, not via QR
