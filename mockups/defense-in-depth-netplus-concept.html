Why does this design fail defense in depth?

Correct. Past the one firewall, everything is flat and the public server is right next to the data. One breach reaches it all. Defense in depth needs more than a single layer.

Look at how many layers stand between the internet and the data. A public server on the flat internal LAN means one control is all that protects everything.

Add the layers this network is missing.

Right. A DMZ isolates the public server so a compromise does not land on the internal network.

Correct answer: a screened subnet (DMZ), so a hit on the public server stays away from internal data.

Right. Segmentation contains lateral movement, which is a layer of its own.

Correct answer: VLAN segmentation. A flat network lets one foothold reach everything.

Right. Monitoring catches what the preventive layers miss.

Correct answer: IDS/IPS. Detection is the layer that sees an attacker who slips past the rest.
